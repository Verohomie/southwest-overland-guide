🟡 Medium Energy · All-Day Story This is the day the trip earns its overlanding title. Instead of a boring highway slab to Kingman, you leave the red rocks of Valley of Fire, cross the Mojave to the Colorado River, then swap pavement for 14 miles of wide, easy desert dirt road through abandoned gold mine country — the Silver Creek Road section of the Arizona Peace Trail. You emerge in Oatman: a genuine 1800s mining ghost town with wild burros wandering the main street, a century-old saloon, and more character than anywhere else on this drive. Then you finish on Historic Route 66 over Sitgreaves Pass — a real mountain road with sweeping views — into Kingman for the night. Four completely different landscapes. Two pieces of American history. One very happy Land Cruiser.
🫏 Why this route instead of the highway?
The straight I-40 option gets you to Kingman faster but gives you nothing — just interstate and truck stops. This route costs 45 extra minutes and gives you: a proper dirt road warm-up for the trip ahead, a ghost town straight out of a western film, wild burros that will literally eat out of your hand, and the most scenic mountain pass on all of Historic Route 66. It's the difference between driving through the Southwest and actually experiencing it — which is the whole point of this trip.

🗺️ The Route — Segment by Segment

Segment 1 — Paved · ~1 hr

Valley of Fire → Bullhead City

Head south on I-15 briefly, then US-93 past Boulder City and the Nevada side of Hoover Dam, dropping into the Colorado River valley at Laughlin/Bullhead City. Fuel up here — this is the last reliable cheap gas until Kingman.

Fuel stop: Bullhead City — top off completely
The drive through the Mojave opens up beautifully in the morning light. The Colorado River appears suddenly as you drop into the valley — genuinely pretty.
Bullhead City AZ
★ Segment 2 — Silver Creek Road (DIRT) · ~1 hr

Bullhead City → Oatman via Silver Creek Road

From Bullhead City, take SR-95 south to Silver Creek Road — when the pavement ends, the adventure begins. This 14-mile graded dirt road is the northern section of the Arizona Peace Trail, passing through Black Mountain gold mine country with sweeping desert views. It's wide enough for two vehicles and regularly maintained. Your Land Cruiser will handle it without breaking a sweat.

Distance: 14 miles of easy dirt · Surface: Wide graded road, two vehicles wide
What you'll see: Active gold mines, abandoned mining ruins, volcanic rock formations, wild burros, panoramic Mojave Desert views to the west
This is your first real dirt road of the trip — a perfect warm-up. Easy enough to relax into, interesting enough to remember. Download the route in OnX or Gaia before leaving Bullhead City as cell service fades.
Silver Creek Rd Start OnX Trail Info
★ Segment 3 — Oatman Stop · 45–60 min

Oatman, AZ — The Ghost Town

You roll into Oatman and immediately understand why people make special trips here. Wild burros wander the wooden sidewalks begging for carrots. The Oatman Hotel has been open since 1902 and still serves food by the fireplace. Old gold mine buildings line the street. It's the real thing — not a theme park.

Must do: Feed the burros (buy hay/carrots at the general store · $1) · Peek inside the Oatman Hotel · Walk the full length of Main Street
Lunch: Oatman Hotel restaurant — open daily, good burgers, cold beer, genuinely historic atmosphere
This is the moment of the day your wife will photograph the most and talk about longest. Burros with their heads in the car window have that effect on people.
Oatman Main Street Oatman Hotel (lunch)
Segment 4 — Historic Route 66 · ~45 min

Oatman → Sitgreaves Pass → Kingman

Leave Oatman heading east on Historic Route 66 — a narrow, winding mountain road that climbs over Sitgreaves Pass at 3,550 feet with spectacular views back across the Mojave. This is genuine old Route 66, unchanged since the 1930s. All paved, but tight and scenic.

Distance: 28 miles paved · Time: ~45 min (slow, scenic mountain road)
The switchbacks over Sitgreaves Pass are the most dramatic piece of paved road on Day 2. Pull off at the top for the view — you can see back to the Colorado River.
Sitgreaves Pass Kingman AZ

🔴 High Adventure East Pocket is one of the most cinematic camping experiences in Arizona. You drive 26 miles of forest service roads through ponderosa pines, and then the trees break — and Sedona's entire red rock wilderness is below you, 2,000 feet down. Arrive by 2 PM to snag a rim site. Your Land Cruiser was built for exactly this.
⚠️ Critical: FR 231 becomes impassable in wet conditions — sticky red clay turns to ice. Check weather the morning of Apr 25. If rain is forecast, stay in Sedona or Flagstaff and try another day.

🗺️ Getting There — Step by Step

Access via Woody Mountain Road (Forest Road 231) from Flagstaff:
1. From Sedona take AZ-89A north to Flagstaff (45 min)
2. In Flagstaff, head west on I-40 to Flagstaff Ranch Road exit
3. Turn south onto Kiltie Lane → Woody Mountain Road (FR 231)
4. Drive FR 231 south for ~26 miles — stay on the main road, ignore all offshoots
5. When trees break on the RIGHT with a cliff view → you've found Edge of the World

Monument Valley — Full POI Guide

The 17-mile Valley Drive is the most iconic landscape in America. The Mittens, Merrick Butte, and Totem Pole are what every John Ford Western, every car commercial, and every road trip fantasy is made of. Your Land Cruiser belongs here.

Free stops (no entry fee):
🎬 Forrest Gump Point — Mile marker 13, Hwy 163. Pull off, stand in the road for 10 seconds, take the photo. Free, 15 min.
👀 Visitor Center Overlook — The iconic Mittens view from the rim. Free to walk up even without paying the loop fee.
Valley Drive loop stops ($8/vehicle — 17 mi, ~2 hrs):
🤠 Mittens & Merrick Butte — Stop 1. The money shot. East + West Mitten Buttes with Merrick behind. Every postcard, every movie. Shoot at sunrise facing east.
🎬 John Ford's Point — Stop 5. Named for the director who made Monument Valley famous. Best panoramic view of the valley. Navajo horse riders available for photos ($2).
🗿 Three Sisters — Stop 4. Three distinct spires, said to represent a nun and two pupils. Distinctive silhouette.
🗼 Totem Pole & Yei Bi Chei — Stop 7. Tallest, most dramatic spire — stands completely alone. Yei Bi Chei resembles Navajo spiritual dancers.
🎨 Artist's Point — Stop 9. Best sunrise spot — faces east. More peaceful than Ford's Point. Panoramic sweep of the whole valley.
🌬️ North Window — Stop 10. Frame East Mitten through the gap between Elephant Butte and Cly Butte. Walk 1/3 mile from parking.
Practical notes: Valley Drive is unpaved dirt — rough but manageable for your LC at low speed. Fee: $8/person. Opens 8am. No reservations needed for self-drive. Navajo guides required for off-loop areas (Hunts Mesa, Mystery Valley). Best light: Sunrise (face east toward Mittens) or golden hour before sunset.
You've driven past Monument Valley on the way to Muley Point. Taking the loop adds 1.5–2 hours and $8. If this is your first time in the Southwest, stop. If you've been before, Forrest Gump Point + Visitor Center Overlook takes 30 minutes and costs nothing.

🔧 Land Cruiser Trail Notes

  • Air down to 25–28 PSI on rocky forest roads (FR 231, Cottonwood Canyon, Moki Dugway)
  • Carry a hi-lift jack, traction boards, and 2-gallon water reserve minimum
  • Recovery gear especially important on FR 231 (Edge of World) — remote, no cell service
  • FR 231 (Edge of World): dry weather only. Clay becomes oil-slick when wet — leave immediately if rain starts
  • Cottonwood Canyon Road: 4x4 strongly recommended. 2WD sedans get stuck regularly in ruts
  • Muley Point Road: accessible in dry conditions to any high-clearance vehicle without 4x4
  • Mescal 4x4 shortcut to Devil's Bridge (Sedona): use FR spur to cut 2 miles and 90% of crowds
  • Schnebly Hill Road (Sedona): Red clay after rain stains permanently — scotchgard undercarriage before trip
  • Grand Canyon East Rim roads (FR 302): generally good gravel, HC helpful but not required

Safe travels. The Southwest earns a permanent place in memory.
